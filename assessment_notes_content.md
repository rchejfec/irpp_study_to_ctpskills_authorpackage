## Set 1: Per-Community Context Paragraphs

These short paragraphs summarize each community's local economic profile and key transition drivers. They can be displayed at the top of the notes panel or merged into tooltips.

### Division No. 3, NL (Channel-Port aux Basques)
> Division No. 3 relies heavily on maritime transport (such as regional ferry operations) and marine harvesting. Long-term transitions face a vulnerable resource base, but are supported locally by municipal public works and infrastructure demand from reconstruction projects.

### Algoma, ON (Sault Ste. Marie)
> Algoma's economy is historically anchored by steel manufacturing. As primary metal industries adopt low-emission technologies, local transition planning leverages regional assets in forestry, metal fabrication, and critical mineral development.

### Oxford, ON (Ingersoll/Woodstock)
> Oxford's economy is integrated with automotive assembly and related manufacturing. Motor vehicle parts manufacturing and regional logistics along the Highway 401 corridor provide alternative transition avenues, alongside southwestern Ontario's broader plastics and metalworking corridors.

### Division No. 15, MB (Neepawa)
> Neepawa has a dual-sector economy built on agriculture and food manufacturing (anchored by major meat packing operations). Local opportunities bridge between agricultural management, food processing, and public infrastructure maintenance.

### Division No. 1, SK (Estevan)
> Estevan relies on a mix of agriculture and energy sectors (coal mining, coal-fired power plants, and oil/gas extraction). As traditional energy generation undergoes transitions, the region leverages a strong transport logistics base (rail corridor), municipal public works, and college training programs.

### Division No. 16, AB (Wood Buffalo)
> Division No. 16 is highly dependent on oilsands extraction and related industrial maintenance, where employment is sensitive to global commodity markets, decarbonization targets, and automation. Transition pathways in the region align with local opportunities in heavy maintenance, welding, electrical servicing, and construction logistics.

### Northwest Territories
> The Northwest Territories has a hybrid economy combining resource extraction (diamond mines and critical minerals) with a large public services sector. Regional pathways focus on maintaining vital community infrastructure, transport networks, and public utilities.
